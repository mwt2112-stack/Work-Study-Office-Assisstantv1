Work-Study Office Assistant

Location: Mary Woodard Lasker Biomedical Research Building, Columbia University Medical Center — Room 540

Position Type: Part-time, Work-Study (Academic Year)

About the Role

The Columbia Engineering administrative team at the Mary Woodard Lasker Biomedical Research Building is seeking a motivated and detail-oriented Work-Study Office Assistant. This position offers hands-on experience supporting research administration, procurement, finance, facilities coordination, and daily office operations within a collaborative academic environment. Comprehensive training is provided, making this an excellent opportunity for students interested in higher education administration, operations, or business support.

Key Responsibilities

Routine Administrative Tasks

  • Greet and assist visitors, students, and faculty in the main office.
  • Maintain cleanliness and organization in shared spaces (office areas, conference rooms, pantry).
  • Receive, organize, track, and distribute incoming mail and laboratory deliveries.
  • Answer general inquiries in person, via email, or by phone.
  • Assist with event preparation and setup when needed.
  • Assist faculty, researchers, and students with package tracking and general ordering inquiries.

Department-Specific Support (Training Provided)

  • Assist with financial reconciliation activities to support departmental purchasing and budget tracking.
  • Support purchasing activities by monitoring orders, updating tracking systems, and assisting with procurement workflows using Quartzy and other university systems.
  • Help maintain departmental directories, FAQs, and training materials.
  • Back up team members in specialized tasks (e.g., event lookups, invoice matching, facilities task follow-ups).

Other Duties as Assigned

  • Assist with updating spreadsheets, databases, and analytics tables.
  • Create quick guides and training documentation for future staff.
  • Develop quick-reference guides and training documentation to support future staff and improve departmental workflows.

Training & Onboarding

Work-Study Assistants follow a structured five-day training program covering:

  • Office tour and introductions to Lasker spaces (offices, labs, student areas, conference rooms, loading dock).
  • Access setup (swipe cards, calendars, vendor logins, departmental folders).
  • Core Administrative Operations: recon, Quartzy ordering, event bookings, package handling.
  • Backup procedures and key contacts for facilities, finance, and deliveries.
  • Independent task management and performance feedback.

Skills & Qualifications

Required

  • Current Columbia University student eligible for Federal Work-Study.
  • Reliable, punctual, and able to work independently after training.
  • Strong communication and organizational skills.
  • Comfortable learning new software systems.
  • Ability to manage multiple tasks while maintaining attention to detail.
  • Comfortable working independently and asking questions when needed.

Preferred

  • Prior office, customer service, or administrative experience.
  • Familiarity with spreadsheets (Google Sheets/Excel).
  • Attention to detail in data entry and record keeping.

Work Schedule & Compensation

  • Flexible hours within standard business times (Mon–Fri, 9 AM–5 PM).
  • Approximately 10–15 hours per week.
  • Hourly wage in accordance with Columbia University work-study rates.
